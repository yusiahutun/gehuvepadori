Firearms Act

Kenya has stringent gun laws and obtaining a license is difficult. Applicants must go through several interviews and background checks.

According to Fire Arms Act Cap 114, no person is allowed to purchase, acquire or have in his possession any firearm or ammunition unless he holds a firearm certificate in force at the time.

The law defines a firearm as a lethal barrelled weapon of any description from which any shot, bullet or other missiles can be discharged or a weapon that can be adapted for the same purpose.

They include a weapon of any description - a stun gun or electronic paralyser- designed or intended to discharge any noxious liquid, noxious gas or other noxious substance or an electrical charge which when it strikes any person or animal is of sufficient strength to stun and temporarily disable the person or animal.

Another weapon described as a firearm is any airgun, air rifle, air pistol, revolver, crossbow, laser gun, or similar weapon.

The barrel, bolt, chamber, silencer, muffler, flash-guard, or any other accessory designed or adapted to diminish the noise or flash caused by firing a weapon and another essential component of any weapon is defined as a firearm.

Lastly, a firearm is any weapon or other device or apparatus that the Minister of Defence may specify by an order published in the Gazette to be a firearm for the Firearms Act's purposes.

Despite the precautions, hundreds of illegal guns are smuggled into the country through porous borders, especially from Somalia, Kenya's war-torn neighbour to the north.

That said, let us look at the five weapons prohibited in the country by the Kenyan government as per the Firearms Act.

1: A firearm designed or adapted in such a way that when pressure is applied to the trigger, missiles are discharged continuously.

This can only be stopped when the applied pressure is removed or the magazine or belt containing the missiles is empty.

Also prohibited is a firearm that has been designed in such a way that for each pressure of the trigger, more than one discharge of a missile can take place is prohibited.

The only exception is when such firearms are modified to the chief licensing officer's satisfaction to ensure that only one missile is discharged for each pressure of the trigger.

2: Any automatic or semi-automatic self-loading military assault rifle of 7.62 mm or 5.56 mm calibre or any other calibre as specified by the Minister of Defense by notice in the Gazette.

3: A firearm fitted with or including any device, accessory or attachment which reduces or is designed to reduce the noise or flash caused by discharging such firearm

This includes any such separate device, accessory or attachment.

4: Any weapon that can be designed or adapted to discharge any noxious liquid, gas or other substance.

There is an exception if only such weapon, noxious liquid, gas, or other substance are of classes or types authorized by the Minister of Defence by notice in the Gazette.

5: Any firearm or ammunition or any class or type of firearm or ammunition or any such device, accessory or attachment described by the Minister of Defence as illegal through a notice in the Gazette.
