"We don't ever have to talk again," Kandi says of her former "RHOA" costar.

Kandi Burruss seems to be inching toward a friendship with Porsha Williams after that explosive "Real Housewives of Atlanta" Season 9 reunion, but a friendship with Phaedra Parks is completely out of the question.

On Sunday's "Watch What Happens Live," a caller asked Kandi, "Now that you and Porsha are in a better place, do you think you and Phaedra will ever come to mends?"

"Nah," Kandi replied instantly. "Absolutely not. Absolutely not. That was too far. That was too much. I've never heard anything [from her]. No apologies, no nothing. And I'm cool with that. We don't ever have to talk again, and I'm good. Let's move forward."

Kandi spent much of Season 9 fighting allegations that she and her husband said they wanted to drug Porsha and take advantage of her. During the reunion, Porsha revealed she first heard about it from Phaedra, who claimed she heard it from Kandi firsthand.

During one of the most emotional reunion episodes in history, Kandi confronted Phaedra about her role in spreading the vicious rape rumor, and Phaedra was subsequently fired from the Bravo series. Since then, Kandi has been fairly cold toward Porsha, who has apologized profusely since the incident. It seems the two are slowly moving forward.

Kandi and Andy Cohen's other guest, Keke Palmer, then played a kinky version of "Never Have I Ever." If the ladies had done the sensual act Andy read off his note card, they were ordered to take a sip from their drinks.
