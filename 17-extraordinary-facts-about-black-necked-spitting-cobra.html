FAQs

1. Are black-necked spitting cobras dangerous?

Yes, black-necked spitting cobras are venomous snakes and should be treated with caution. Their venom can cause severe symptoms and even be fatal to humans if not treated promptly.

2. Can black-necked spitting cobras spit venom accurately?

Yes, black-necked spitting cobras have an impressive ability to spit venom with accuracy. They can accurately hit their target up to two meters away, which serves as a defense mechanism against potential threats.

3. What do black-necked spitting cobras eat?

Black-necked spitting cobras primarily feed on small mammals, birds, and reptiles. They are skilled hunters and use their venom to immobilize and digest their prey.

4. How long do black-necked spitting cobras live?

On average, black-necked spitting cobras live for about 10-15 years in the wild. However, in captivity, they can live up to 20 years or more with proper care and conditions.

5. Where can black-necked spitting cobras be found?

Black-necked spitting cobras are mainly found in sub-Saharan Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, and South Africa. They inhabit various habitats, including forests, grasslands, and even urban areas.
