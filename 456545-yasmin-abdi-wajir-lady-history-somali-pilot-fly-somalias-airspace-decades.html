Yasmin Abdi: Wajir Lady Makes History as First Somali Pilot to Fly in Somalia's Airspace in Decades

October 09, 2024

  • Amin exuded confidence that she was equal to the task and had the potential to fly people across the country

Yasmin Abdi Farah has beaten all odds to shine in a career mostly shunned by Somali women because of the conservative culture and the austerity of the Islamic Sharia.

Yasmin became the first female pilot in Somalia on Wednesday, May 25, as a full captain since the collapse of the central Somali government in 1991.

Somalia's Security Sector Reform tweet showed Yasmin flying from Mogadishu to Kismayo on her maiden flight as a fully-fledged captain for Mandeeq Airlines.

"I had several landings at this airport, but I was always the first officer in the right seat," added Yasmin.

Yasmin Abdi Farah congratulated

The pilot, who hails from Wajir county, flew with many captains to the region of Somalia for five years as the first officer.

One of her instructors, captain Osman M. Amin, said that he was excited that Yasmin graduated and got her wings today. He said she is a role model for other young Somali girls.

“Today, I am very excited to see Yasmin become a full pilot after years of rigorous training and being a first officer in the crew. It is now on her to discharge her responsibilities," he said.

Passengers aboard the flight were also pleased to witness a female captain.

"I am 78 years old; today it is my first time to see a female pilot, I wish Yasmin all the best, and she is a Somali girl, just like my daughters."
