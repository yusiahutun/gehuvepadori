P'Trique's List - 24 Hours With P'Trique

March 30, 2024
blog

8:01 A.M. My iPhone alarm clock goes off. Save for my personalized Chanel sleep mask and my "youth" wraps (a mixture of aloe, olive oil, and açaí berry on strips of Egyptian silk), I sleep nude. As with a bottle of 1986 Lafite Rothschild, you have to let your body breathe. I'm staying at the Bowery Hotel for Fashion Week. It is like my fourth home in New York.

8:13 A.M. I lie still, meditating, until my assistant arrives. Fashion Week gets crazy, so I try my best to always find a moment to center. My assistant and I do not speak as she removes my wraps. She has brought my breakfast—a chilled shot of espresso and the bottom half of a gluten-free lemon-poppy-seed muffin. I despise muffin tops, edible or otherwise. Though I'm a regular practitioner of P90X, Bikram yoga, Cardio Barre, and the Brazilian Butt Lift workout, I do not go to the gym during Fashion Week. There just isn't enough time. Instead I do the first 30 days of the Insanity workout all in the week prior.

9:31 A.M. My first show isn't until noon, but I must allow two hours to complete my daily beauty regimen. Petra, my hairstylist, comes over to tszuj my bangs. I was in Mustique a few weeks ago, so my skin is already perfectly bronzy.

10:29 A.M. Since I am a complex, multidimensional being, I insist that my signature aroma reflect that. Therefore, I layer a carefully chosen selection of eight perfumes on different body parts and pulse points, including a spritz with Chance by Chanel and a walk-through with Tom Ford's Urban Musk.

11:01 A.M. My bestie (known to many as Wang Girl, after Alexander, natch) arrives in an all-black ensemble. She's a retired model, sample size, and has been on the Sartorialist six times.

11:30 A.M. We're in the town car on the way to the Prabal Gurung show. Just as I layer my perfumes, I layer my designers. I'm wearing my vintage YSL Mondrian dress, a Rodarte for Opening Ceremony mohair tarantula sweater, a Lanvin turban, and Prada Flame shoes.

11:55 A.M. The only person I allow to take my photo inside the actual shows is Bill Cunningham, so I pause en route to our seat to allow a few snaps. The show is like a series of flashbacks through my early 20s: harnesses, cutouts, headpieces. I clutch Ken Downing's hand all through the finale. Did I just see my wedding dress go by? Yes, yes, I did.

1:20 P.M. Back at the Bowery, I pass out facedown for a 30-minute disco nap. When I wake up, I have a champers pick-me-up and dash off to the next show.

2:40 P.M. In the town car on my way to Lincoln Center with Wang Girl for Hervé Léger. As much as je deteste the continuing saga of the bandage dress, I must support my old friend Max Azria. I am wearing a YSL burnt-sienna gown and a vintage fur coat with my Stella McCartney clutch.

4:20 P.M. In the car on our way to the Wang show, I change into my white mirror-and-sequin-embroidered Chloé tunic and fur jacket.

5:01 P.M. We're in our seats. Wang Girl is, obviously, beside herself. And with good reason: The #totesamaze, anti-eating mouth cages, leather and fringe details, and dominatrix coats are all to die for. Gisele winks at me just before her runway turn.

6:30 P.M. I completely forgot about lunch (it's a habit), so I'm back at the Bowery with Derek Blasberg for a hydration and nourishment session on my balcony. We girl-talk and take Instagram photos.

8:10 P.M. I'm supposed to be at Altuzarra, but I got a little too tipsy. Such is life.

9:30 P.M. I change into my vintage blue silk Stella McCartney dress with a beaded silver Stella clutch and a beaded vintage blue duster, all topped with an ultrachic tiny hat. It's an avant-garde Downton Abbey moment. I idiotically released my driver in a fit of champagne, so I'm trying to hail a cab to get to the Max Azria after-party. Cobblestones are the bane of my existence, especially when I'm wearing cab-to-curb heels.

12:00 A.M. I'm with Cory Kennedy and Mr. Blasberg in the infamous crawl space of Le Baron. We're inspired by all the oxblood tones on the runways, so we're drinking red wine.

4:32 A.M. Against all odds, I complete my beauty routine and make it into bed. I remove my makeup; slather a layer of Crème de la Mer over my face, neck, and décolletage; fire off a good-night tweet to my followers; and apply my youth wraps. I always sleep on my back to evenly distribute gravity and avoid wrinkling. Good night!

As told to Celeste Hughey and Jonathon Burford
