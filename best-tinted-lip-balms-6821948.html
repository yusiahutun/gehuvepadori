The 10 Best Tinted Lip Balms of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

August 12, 2024
blog

Honestly, we live for a great tinted lip balm. We're always on the hunt for a hydrating, juicy wash of color that won't dry out our pout the way long wear lipsticks tend to do. And with these natural-looking balms in hues of pinks, reds, and nudes, you don't have to sacrifice one bit of long-lasting hydration.

Considering there are so many tinted lip balm options — and many of them are great formulas — it was difficult to pare down our beloved tinted lip balms to only our very favorites, as featured on this list. To provide you with a highly curated list, our team of InStyle editors tested dozens of tinted lip balms, from the buzziest, most popular options to the tried-and-true stalwarts, and judged each one for color payoff, hydration, longevity, and the overall way it looked on our lips. From there, we collected all the data and ranked each product. The top scoring tinted lip balms earned a spot on our winners list.

Best Overall

NARS Afterglow Lip Balm

Sephora

What We Love: The balmy texture and subtle shimmer makes this look more elevated than just a lip balm.

What We Don’t Love: You'll need to reapply often to maintain the color.

We loved the texture of this balm — it’s not too waxy but not too slippery either, and it stayed on our lips for a solid amount of time. It hydrated our lips after just one application (so we didn't find ourselves needing to constantly reapply throughout the day, except when we wanted a bit more color) and left them soft long after we swiped it on.

We love that some of the hues come with a bit of subtle shimmer, which can look very sexy when done right. And all of the colors are very wearable, although none are super bold or bright. Each shade offers a sheer wash of color in a way that might be hard for anyone to tell if you have a lip product on or not. In other words, they look very natural on the lips.

Size: 0.1 oz | Shades: 9 | Scented: No

Best Drugstore

ChapStick Total Hydration Moisture + Tint

Walgreens

What We Love: It's equal parts hydrating and colorful.

What We Don’t Love: If you have chronically dry lips, you'll need to use a more nourishing balm in tandem.

The first time we tried these tinted lip balms, we were teenagers, but it was love at first swipe. These drugstore lipsticks deliver surprisingly vibrant payoff, especially considering how affordable they are. And every one of the nine shades somehow looks great on our lips — and we tested them on skin tones ranging from the very light to the very deep, and they were flattering for all.

One of the downsides of this formula is that it’s not as moisturizing as some of the other picks on this list. Additionally, if your lips are dehydrated to begin with, get them back to neutral before using this balm because the color can slide and settle into dry cracks. Our solution was to first apply a sheer lip balm underneath, and then add the ChapStick balm on top. We also started to use a hydrating overnight lip oil to manage our parched lips, and our situation turned right around.

Size: 0.12 oz | Shades: 9 | Scented: No

Best Splurge

Yves Saint Laurent Rouge Volupté Shine Lipstick Balm

Sephora

What We Love: With 30 juicy colors to choose from, you'll never run out of options.

What We Don’t Love: The brighter hues may not look natural on some skin tones.

This lipstick delivers in every way. The sleek packaging is just the beginning. With 30 stunning shades, from bold to barely-there, you'll have a hard time choosing just one. "No matter how many lipsticks I buy, I always return to this one in the shade 44 Nude Lavalliere daily," says commerce writer Irene Richardson. "The versatile color goes with everything I own and, frankly, it’s just a real treat to apply."

But the real magic lies in the formula. Silky-smooth and packed with six nourishing oils, it pampers your lips with luxurious shine and long-lasting comfort. Antioxidant powerhouses like pomegranate extract and macadamia butter keep them feeling soft and conditioned. While it offers more pigment than a typical lip balm, it glides on effortlessly and leaves lips looking plump and beautiful, even after the color fades. "Swiping it on feels as luxurious as the brand itself — the formula is super lightweight and creamy, and it smells fantastic (like a fancy department store)," says Richardson.

Size: 0.11 oz | Shades: 30 | Scented: Yes

Best Clean

rms beauty Hydrating Tinted Daily Lip Balm

Sephora

What We Love: It offers a nice, subtle color with a formula made with clean ingredients. 

What We Don’t Love: It doesn’t last super long on the lips. 

Rms beauty did a fantastic job creating their roster of high-performance products with clean ingredients — and the Hydrating Tinted Daily Lip Balm is a great example. This pick comes in nine shades, including pinks, reds, oranges, and plums. Thanks to beeswax, the texture feels waxier than it does slick. It’s also soy-free, gluten-free, cruelty-free, and fragrance-free, too. Sometimes, our sensitive lips can get irritated from fragrances, but we didn't have to worry about that when applying this to our lips. 

One of the downsides of this product is that it doesn’t stay on the lips for very long. However, hardly any lip balm will stay locked in place for as long as a liquid lipstick or lip stain might, so it's easy to look past.

Size: 0.10 oz | Shades: 9 | Scented: No

Best Moisturizing

Keys Soulcare Comforting Tinted Lip Balm

Ulta Beauty

What We Love: The formula is the lip balm equivalent of an intensely moisturizing body butter.

What We Don’t Love: The shade range could be better.

Need an ultra-hydrating formula to get you through colder weather? Try Keys Soulcare Comforting Tinted Lip Balm. This formula feels seriously conditioning without traces of stickiness, dryness, or tackiness, thanks to the included avocado and camellia seed oil, which deeply nourish the lips. The shade range could use a little innovation, though. We wish the berry-heavy range featured a pink or a blue-toned red in order to complement more undertones.

Size: 0.1 oz | Shades: 6 | Scented: No

Best Natural Finish

Ilia Balmy Tint Hydrating Lip Balm

Credo Beauty

What We Love: Both the shade range and the balm's slick texture are a dream.

What We Don’t Love: The round bullet makes for an imprecise application.

ILIA's Balmy Tint Hydrating Lip Balm offers a wide range of colors, impressing celebrity makeup artist Delina Medhin. The eight shades flatter all skin tones with a natural finish, while hydrating ingredients keep lips soft. "I really this lip balm in the Wanderlust shade," says Chloe Anello, former senior commerce editor at InStyle. "I actually didn't know it was a lip balm when I first got it because it's pigmented like a lipstick. I love how creamy and hydrating it is, but it gives a bit more than a subtle flush of color. I got it for my mom, too, who loves it, as well."

Word to the wise: The round bullet means you definitely need a mirror to apply this one. And the buttery formula makes it a bit slippery. If you do mess up, though — happens to the best of us — it’s easy to wipe away without ruining the rest of your makeup.

Size: 0.15 oz | Shades: 8 | Scented: Yes

Best Long-Lasting

Chanel Les Beiges Healthy Glow Lip Balm

Nordstrom

What We Love: The striking colors last longer on lips than many other lip balms.

What We Don’t Love: It's not the most hydrating option on this list.

A tinted lip balm is nowhere near as long-lasting as, say, a matte liquid lipstick. But there are certainly tinted lip balms that will last longer than others, like Chanel Les Beiges Healthy Glow Lip Balm. Not only does it come in five very flattering shades, but it also has a great color payoff, too, which stays on lips longer than some other options on this list. Additionally, the formula leans more waxy than buttery, proving that it will stay on your lips longer than a thinner consistency might. 

Medhin calls this balm “buildable” because “you can really intensify the color payoff,” she explains. Although a waxy formula doesn't deliver waves of hydration, it's still moisturizing enough to be comfortable while adding beautiful color to the lips.   

Size: 0.1 oz | Shades: 5 | Scented: Yes

Best Color Payoff

Sunnies Face Lip Treat

Sunnies Face

What We Love: Each shade is extremely pigmented.

What We Don’t Love: The shiny gloss wears off after a while.

The Sunnies Face Lip Treat offers a moisturizing, shiny formula that delivers a solid dose of pigment as well. Although there are six hues to choose from, they're all in the neutral family, so if you're looking for a bright coral or a Barbie pink, you're not going to get it here.

Thanks to shea butter and collagen, this pick really kept our lips moisturized after just one swipe. The colors for each balm definitely appear vivid on lips, and the balmy texture also offers a really lovely shine without the stickiness of a lip gloss. But as this is a balm and not an actual gloss, don't expect the glossiness to last longer than an hour between applications.

Size: 0.10 oz | Shades: 6 | Scented: No

Pat McGrath Labs

"When it comes to a hydrating and subtle pigment for the lips, you can’t go wrong with Pat McGrath’s Lip Fetish Sheer Colour Lip Balm," says Jasmine Hyman, a writer and producer for InStyle. "Infused with hydrating shea butter and vitamin E, the formula imparts color with a glossy sheen, while also keeping the lips moisturized all day long."

This lip balm combines buildable, non-sticky color with a comfortable, hydrating formula. Swipe it on once for a subtle hint of tint, or layer it up for richer color. We especially love the creamy shades, with Hyman highlighting "Blow Up," a peachy coral that enhances her natural lip color.

Dior Addict Lip Glow

It just doesn’t get more iconic than the Dior Addict Lip Glow. I mean, are you even an It Girl if you don’t have this floating around somewhere in your purse? We love this balm (just like everyone else) because of its large, inclusive shade range of 23 hues that can suit all skin tones. But as lush as the colors look in the packaging, each of the shades goes on sheer to give your lips a slight flush. One mega fan is Alyssa Kingham, production manager at InStyle, who swears by the hue 020 Mahogany, a brown-berry hybrid.

What to Keep In Mind

  • Hydration: According to celebrity makeup artist Mark de los Reyes, hydration is key. “We want our lips to be supple and hydrated,” he says. “So make sure to drink your water and hydrate from the inside out —then you can pick out a lip balm that can support and retain all that moisture.” Adding a good lip scrub to your regimen can also help. Look for ingredients like shea butter and vitamin E, which can be found in majority of these lip balms on this list, to make the most of your lippie.
  • Opacity: The opacity of your tinted lip balm is a personal preference — do you want a balm that is super sheer with just a hint of color, or something that’s a little more buildable? “I tend to opt for a lip balm that can give me a wash of color yet buildable enough for a sheer-medium coverage,” says de los Reyes. “This allows me just enough color while keeping the natural nuance of the lip visible.
  • Texture: Another thing to consider when purchasing a tinted lip balm is the texture of the formula — there are waxy semi-matte balms, silky oils, and juicy jellies. “The finishing texture is your story to tell. How do you want your lips to look and feel?” asks de los Reyes. “Maybe for a night out, try playing with an oil-based balm that has a little more shine and a smoothing silky texture.”

Your Questions, Answered

Can you use tinted lip balms overnight? 

Both Medhin and de los Reyes agree that using a tinted lip balm isn’t harmful for your lips, but might not be ideal for your pillowcases. “I typically do not use tinted lip balms overnight because I wouldn't want the color to transfer to my pillowcase,” explains Medhin. “For overnight wear, I would suggest a clear lip treatment.” Another great overnight option is lip masks.

Although if you’re really desperate, wearing a tinted lip balm to bed is fine. “Let’s just say you were in a pinch and you were left with just the solitary tinted lip balm that’s been in your bag all day, and there was no way you were going to sleep without hydrating those lips,” says de los Reyes. “It would be perfectly fine to apply and blot the excess color away with a tissue, and then get to bed.”

Is it possible to over-use lip balms?

It’s debatable! “I’m personally addicted to keeping my own lips moisturized, and I’m aware that the more I use balms, the more my lips may feel as if they’re dependent on that daily or hourly swipe of moisture,” says de los Reyes. “This is why it’s important to go for products that will nourish and moisturize your lips if you apply your lip balms habitually — this way, if you do over-use, would it really be so wrong?”
