As a celebrated actress and director, Sally Field has lit up both the big and small screens and charmed millions of viewers for the last six decades. Field embarked on her acting journey at a young age, making her television debut in the 1960s hit series Gidget and The Flying Nun. However, it was her remarkable ability to portray complex, relatable characters that catapulted her to stardom and earned her critical acclaim.

Field’s breakthrough came in the 1979 film Norma Rae, where she played the titular character, a fearless textile worker fighting for workers’ rights. Her powerful performance in the film not only earned her the Academy Award for Best Actress, but also established her as a force to be reckoned with in Hollywood.
