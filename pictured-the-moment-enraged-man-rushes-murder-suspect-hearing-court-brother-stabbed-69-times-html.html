A courtroom in Massachusetts erupted into chaos Monday when the brother of a murder victim attacked the man accused of stabbing his sibling 69 times.

Peter Deconinck, 46, arrived in Chelsea District Court to be arraigned for the murder of Ronald Russo, 50, who was found stabbed to death inside a blood-spattered mobile home at Lee's Trailer Park in Revere Saturday.

As a prosecutor was reading the grisly details of the crime, Russo's brother, 51-year-old Thomas Russo, became enraged and rushed at Deconinck.

Defendant: Peter Deconinck, 46, pleaded not guilty to the murder of Ronald Russo

Mr Russo put up a fight against four court officers who attempted to restrain him while shouting at his brother‘s accused killer.

A witness told the Boston Herald that he heard Mr Russo cry out, 'sixty nine wounds ain't right,' as he was being dragged out of the courtroom.

One of the court officers sustained injuries while trying to break up the brawl and was seen being wheeled out of the court on a gurney.

Thomas Russo on Tuesday pleaded not guilty to two counts of resisting arrest and one count of disrupting court proceedings.

'Listening to the events of the alleged murder he was just very incensed,' said Jay Satin, Tom Russo's attorney, according to the station WHDH.

He was then released on his own recognizance and ordered by a judge to stay away from the defendant’s family, the Boston Globe reported.

 

Held back: Four court officers went after Russo trying to pin him down

Emotional hearing: Other family members of the victim wept in court as a prosecutor was describing the murder

Aftermath: One court officer had to be wheeled out of the courtroom after sustaining injuries in the melee

The brutal slaying took place sometime before 8.30pm Saturday when Deconinck and Russo got into an alcohol-fueled fight , according to investigators.

Deconinck, Russo and a third man had spent hours drinking inside a trailer. At one point, the unnamed friend stepped out, only to discover the two men fighting upon his return.

Each of the brawlers had a knife in his hand, according to their companion, who fled the trailer fearing for his life.

Deconinck was arrested near the MBTA Beachmont station later that night. According to police, the man had 'blood on his clothing and a laceration to his hand.'

Police then went to the trailer in the 400 block of Revere Beach Parkway, where they discovered 50-year-old Russo dead in a blood-stained room, still clutching a knife in his hand. According to officials, the man was stabbed 69 times.

Violent confrontation: Ronald Russo and Peter Deconinck were drinking inside a trailer in Revere when they got into a fight

Grisly scene: A man who discovered Russo's body covered with 69 stab wounds said that the interior of the mobile home was spattered with blood

Deconinck denied killing Russo and said that even if he did, it was in self-defense, adding that he was either shot or stabbed in his calf, CBS Boston reported. According to police, the suspect was belligerent and allegedly made verbal threats against the arresting officers.

The 46-year-old entered a not guilty plea and was ordered held on $1million cash bail.  He is due back in court September 26.

Witnesses said Saturday the crime scene where Russo’s body was discovered was horrific, the station NECN reported.

‘There was blood everywhere, the ceilings, walls, tables upside down, chairs upside down,’ said James Harriman, who found the body.
