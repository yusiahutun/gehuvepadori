Sofia Kenin is an American professional tennis player who is slowly climbing up in the WTA Rankings. Sofia rose to fame when she won her first-ever grand slam in the Australian Open 2020 and ever since then she has been improvising her game.

She played in Wimbledon 2023 where she knocked out 7th-ranked Coco Gauff in the first round. Early Life

Sofia Kenin was born on November 14, 1998, in Moscow, Russia, to parents Alexander and Svetlana Kenin. Just a few months after her birth, the Kenin family moved to the United States. They settled in New York City with only $298 to their name. Luckily, their relatives stepped in to offer vital support during this challenging time, allowing Sofia to grow up with her sister, Anya.

Sofia had a strong passion for tennis from a young age, and it became the central focus of her life. Although details about her high school and college education aren’t readily available, her dedication to tennis has always been clear.

Sofia Kenin Career

Sofia Kenin’s tennis journey took her to the professional level, where she achieved a remarkable ranking of No. 2 in the ITF junior rankings. She began competing in lower-level Grade 4 events on the ITF Junior Circuit when she was just 13 years old in 2012. After securing her first titles in both singles and doubles in 2013, she moved up to the more competitive Grade 1 level.

Towards the end of that year, she made her Grade A debut at the Orange Bowl, where she reached the semifinals in singles and finished as the runner-up in doubles, partnering with Kaitlyn McCarthy. They were narrowly defeated by Tornado Alicia Black and Naiktha Bains.

Widespread Fame

Sofia Kenin stepped into low-level ITF competitions in 2013, securing her first two professional wins at just 14 years old. Her triumph at the USTA junior national title granted her a wildcard, granting her a chance to make her Grand Slam debut at the 2015 US Open. Unfortunately, she lost her first match to Mariana Duque-Mariño.

The following year marked a turning point for Kenin as she clinched her first two ITF titles. The initial victory came at a $25K event in Wesley Chapel, Florida, followed by another win at a $50K event in Sacramento, California. This second title earned her the US Open Wild Card Challenge win, granting her a wildcard entry into the main draw of the US Open for the second time.

Although her journey at the US Open ended with a loss in the opening round against Karolína Plíšková, it was her sole WTA Tour-level match of the year. With an improved ranking, Kenin could focus more on the WTA Tour in 2018. She began the year on a high note, reaching her first WTA quarterfinal at the Auckland Open.

Ups & Down

After facing defeats in all five of her WTA Tour matches on clay, Sofia Kenin had a breakthrough on grass. She reached her first WTA semifinal at the Mallorca Open, a significant achievement on this surface. Kenin pulled off a notable upset against the favored world No. 6 Caroline Garcia, marking her first professional win against a top-ten player. 

Continuing her momentum in the grass-court season, Kenin made her mark at Wimbledon with a second-round appearance. Notably, she secured her debut win at the prestigious event, defeating Maria Sakkari. This series of successes demonstrated her adaptability and growing prowess on different surfaces.
