How To Bathe Your Dog During Winter: 9 Steps To Follow

June 16, 2024
blog

Warmth is the key to winter dog washes. From regulating the right temperature to drying their fur, there are plenty of ways owners can keep dogs clean and cosy when the chilly season arrives.

"During winter it is essential that dogs are bathed in the safest and most comfortable way. A warm bath can create a favourable affiliation towards water and the bathtub. It also allows those frosty morning baths to become a lot easier for the dog, and more manageable for the owner," Martin Smith, owner of Showerstoyou.co.uk, says.

"Ensuring great care is taken with the temperature, monthly baths can be great as they encourage dogs to produce their distinctive scent, which they use to communicate with other animals, and lowers their risk of getting a chill."

Take a look at the tips for bathing your dog in winter...

1. Bathe your dog in the morning

The biggest concern with bathing your dog in the winter is the possibility of hypothermia, so it's important to keep them warm.

The specialists at Showerstoyou.co.uk explain: "It's recommended to bathe your dog in the morning in winter, so they can dry off in the afternoon when the sun has reached its warmest. This prevents them from having a wet coat in the evening when it's typically colder."

Cavan Images//Getty Images

2. Pay close attention to their paws

Your dog's paws may need more attention during the winter months, as nails might collect salt or snow on outdoor walks.

"Examining your dog's claws frequently is more important because in the winter, as your pet isn't as active, you may notice that their nails seem to grow longer. Long nails make it difficult for dogs to retain a firm hold in the bath which can lead to them falling over."

3. Brushing your dog before and after the bath

According to the experts, it's best to brush your dog's fur before and after bathing them to remove any loose fur and debris. "All dog breeds shed occasionally, so it's advisable to remove any extra dead hair before shampooing," the team say.

"Before bathing, brushing your pet's hair will help remove any matting and tangles, making the process simpler and more enjoyable for both you and your dog."

4. Use cotton balls to clean ears

It's common for dogs to get water in their ears, but if left unattended, this could lead to an ear infection. To prevent water from getting into your pup's ears, block their external ear canal with a large cotton ball before bath time — just don't forget to remove it after your pet leaves the water.

"Dogs dislike getting water in their ears, therefore they may shake off mid-bath in an effort to get the water out. This can be avoided by inserting a little cotton ball into your pet's ears during bathing, making the experience more relaxing for your dog."

Keep It 100//Getty Images

5. Adjust the temperature

"It's not recommended to give your dog a cold bath as it's bad for their health. It's best to slightly warm the water in winter, this way they will be comfortable, feel relaxed and enjoy their bath," the experts say.

"It is crucial to be careful with the water temperature and ensure it is 37°C / 98.6°. For slightly lower to avoid burning them, especially with puppies and elderly dogs as water that is excessively hot can strain your pet's heart."

6. Don't get water in their nose

Water can get up a dog's nose from time to time. While it doesn't cause any harm, it can be an uncomfortable experience for them. To avoid this, lift up their head slightly and pay close attention to the nose. If there is too much water inside the nostrils, the risk of choking increases.

7. Choose the right shampoo

When it comes to selecting a canine shampoo, consider your dog's skin type. If your dog's skin dries out easily, look for a moisturising shampoo. To lower the risk of an allergic response, the RSPCA advises trying a small amount of shampoo on your dog first to test if there is any reaction.

Don't forget to check the ingredients before buying, paying close attention to chemicals and added scents.

8. Rinse and repeat

The team say: "Bathing your dog doesn't have to be a drawn-out process. Rinsing your dog off fast but thoroughly is important to prevent leftover suds which can irritate your dog's skin. Rinsing quickly also means that your dog spends less time exposed to the chilly air."

9. Dry your dog

Dogs are unable to regulate their temperatures in the same way we do, so getting your furry friend dry after bath time is very important — especially during the winter months.

"After you have finished bathing your dog, blow-dry them with a hairdryer set to the lowest heat setting. The dog's coat will be warmed and fluffed, keeping them comfortable as they dry. Holding the dog in place with one hand, move the blow dryer back and forth over the body while keeping the nozzle at least six inches away to avoid burns."
