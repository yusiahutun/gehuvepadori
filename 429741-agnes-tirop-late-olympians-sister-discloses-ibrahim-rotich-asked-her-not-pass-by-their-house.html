Agnes Tirop: Late Olympian's Sister Discloses Ibrahim Rotich Asked Her Not to Pass by Their House

July 01, 2024
blog

  • On Wednesday, October 13, the athlete was found dead in her Iten home
  • Rotich was arrested on Thursday, October 14, in connection with her death

Police have launched a manhunt for an unidentified woman who reportedly visited Agnes Tirop's home in Iten, Elgeyo Marakwet county just a few hours before she was found dead.

Keiyo North police boss Tom Makori said detectives had taken CCTV installation from their home for analysis.

Woman held talks with Rotich

The deceased's younger sister Everlyne Chepng'etich who lived with her revealed that the woman held talks with Tirop's husband, Emmanuel Ibrahim Rotich, on Tuesday, October 12, morning.

"There was a knock on the gate on Tuesday around 8am. I opened and came face to face with the woman who entered without uttering a word. She talked to Rotich for about five minutes before leaving," Daily Nation quoted Chepng'etich

Commotion in bedroom

Chepng'etich said Tirop did not come out of the house on the said date and suspects she could have been killed on Monday, October 11, night as she heard a commotion in the master bedroom.

However, Rotich told her she was well and sent her to buy meat but since she had other errands she left the house.

"At about Ipm, Rotich called, inquiring if I had returned. He asked me not to pass by the house, claiming he and Agnes had left for Nairobi," she recounted.

On Tuesday evening, Chepng'etich told her parents about Tirop's absence.

They reported the matter to Iten Police Station.

Tirop's death

On Wednesday, October 13, she was found dead in the house with stab wounds in the abdomen and neck.

Chepmwetich said her sister had fears for her life after she was abused on several occasions by the suspect.

Rotich arrested

On Thursday, October 15, evening, the husband of the slain athlete was arrested in Mombasa while trying to flee the country.

According to a statement from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), Rotich was arrested in Changamwe while trying to evade the long arm of the law.

The investigative agency said Rotich had rammed his getaway vehicle into a lorry at Mlolongo, along Mombasa Road, as he tried to evade the detectives' dragnet.
