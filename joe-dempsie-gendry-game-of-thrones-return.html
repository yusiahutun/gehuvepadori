Joe Dempsie on Gendry's return to Game of Thrones

September 26, 2024
blog

Monday night saw Gendry's grand return to Game of Thrones after a four-season absence (he was last seen rowing off in a boat all the way back in season three), and no one was more pleased to see him make a comeback than Joe Dempsie.

"It was quite a considerable sabbatical," he told us when we met in London. "At the end of season three the show creators said he was going to disappear for a while, but not to worry because they would bring him back. The idea was that they didn't know how long he was going to be gone for, but they wanted him to be gone long enough that people started forgetting about him, so that when he did return it would be a surprise and of considerable importance.

"Ironically enough I think what's happened is almost the opposite. Because it's a show that has such an obsessive group of fans that will pour over every tiny detail, every loose end that's left untied becomes more and more conspicuous the longer it goes on."

Joe Dempsie as Gendry in 'Game of Thrones'. HBO

So was there ever a point where he started to doubt Gendry's return?

"My mum would at various points say to me, 'Do you think you should email David [Benioff] and Dan [Weiss, the show's creators]?' Like they were going to see an email from me and think, 'Oh my god. We've had this whole story planned out but now we'll change it because you've sent us an email.' So I just had to sit back. It's amazing being part of something like Game of Thrones, but what comes with that is an understanding that it's bigger than you. It's bigger than any character and any actor. I'm very aware that Gendry could be killed at any moment with one line â€“ he could have just drowned at sea and never returned."

"It's amazing being part of something like 'Game of Thrones', but what comes with that is an understanding that it's bigger than you"

Dempsie, who first found fame as the loveable Chris Miles in E4's boundary-breaking TV series Skins, added that having time away from Game of Thrones really allowed him to appreciate what an incredible phenomenon it is.

"That's been the interesting thing for me, having been out of the show for years and then coming back into it, I was able to take a step back," he explained. "With anything you're actually involved with, when you're in the eye of the storm, it's kind of hard to gauge the impact and the size of it - until you step away. I think sometimes something needs to be taken away from you to make you really appreciate it.

"I sort of felt one stepped removed from it going back in, so I was able to really enjoy the hysteria for what it is - and really savour it. I think the cast are very aware at the moment that the end is nigh, so the feeling is very much 'drink it in while you can'."

"Sometimes something needs to be taken away from you to make you really appreciate it"

Another huge difference coming back to the show in season seven? "Half your mates are dead," he laughed. "But it's just been so great to be back on set and seeing some old faces. I was just really looking forward to seeing where Gendry was going to go"

Speaking of which, it turns out that Gendry wasn't the first character that Dempsie auditioned for. Nor was he the second. Or the third.

"Like a lot of the male members of the cast I had actually auditioned for the part of Jon Snow way, way back," he told us. "There was an 18-month period between shooting the pilot and then shooting the rest. Then when the chance came up to audition for the rest of the parts I decided to go for it. I actually auditioned for three other parts before eventually landing the role of Gendry."

And fans everywhere are relieved that he did.
