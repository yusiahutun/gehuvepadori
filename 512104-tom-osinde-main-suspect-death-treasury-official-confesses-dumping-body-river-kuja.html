Tom Osinde: Main Suspect In Death of Ex-Treasury Official Confesses to Dumping Body in River Kuja

September 16, 2024
blog
  • Julius Mugoi is believed to be the last person to be seen driving Tom Osinde's car two days after he was reported missing
  • After his arrest, Mugoi led detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations to River Kuja, where he alleged to have dumped Osinde's remains
  • Mugoi is one of the employees working at the Nakuru farm of the former National Treasury official

Migori - The body of former National Treasury official Tom Osinde has been found dumped in a river, marking the end of a frantic search for the former state official that had entered its 10th day.

It has emerged that the Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations made a breakthrough after two employees of Osinde were arrested.

On Wednesday, June 28, police arrested Julius Mugoi and Mugo Gathii concerning the disappearance of the former Treasury official, Citizen TV reported.

Julius Mugoi confesses facts about Osinde's death

According to Nyakeyo area chief in South Mugirango, Mugoi, who is being treated as the main suspect, confessed to having dumped the body in River Kuja.

The chief revealed that the suspect led the detectives to the river and showed them where he allegedly dumped the body.

Police, with the help of area residents, searched for the body, but they could find it until late in the night.

"This river behind me is called River Kuja . Here is where we came with detectives from DCI to where the main suspect Julius Mogoi said he had dumped the body of Osinde. We marshalled residents to help in the search but we were unsuccessful during the day, the chief said.

Mugoi was reportedly seen driving Osinde's black Toyota Land Cruiser in Kisii county two days before the vehicle was found abandoned in Trans Mara West, Narok county.

According to the residents, the suspect had fled the area three years ago and had an assortment of household goods inside the car.

Tom Osinde's family confirms report

Meanwhile, police officers involved in the search disclosed that the body was already decomposing when it was found.

According to the police report, the body was found in River Kuja on June 28 and taken to the mortuary.

The deceased's brother, Enock Nemwel Osinde, confirmed the reports saying the family had positively identified the body.

“Thank you, friends, for your outpouring of love, prayers, and support during my brother's search. Unfortunately, the outcome isn't the one we anticipated. Tom was killed and his body was found in Migori County. Please continue praying for us as we try to come to terms,” Enock said.
