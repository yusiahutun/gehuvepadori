A jumpy young racehorse made a memorable first-ever start after throwing his rider and escaping a racetrack in Florence, Kentucky, on Saturday night.

Joseph The Catfish had to be rescued by officials near a ramp to an interstate highway after jumping the fence at Turfway Park and then cantering past a Target store and a steakhouse.

The loose horse had thrown jockey Eddie Zuniga on the second bend of the track before fleeing the arena. Luckily he was corralled some distance away with only bumps and bruises.

Going: The young horse spots an escape route

Representatives at the Turfway Park racetrack say Joseph seemed jittery from the very start of his first race, reported Fox19.

Zuniga was able to coax the three-year-old horse to the second corner, where he decided he had had enough. Joe separated from the field, bucked his rider and headed for the exit.

Once he had jumped over the barriers of the track, the horse headed down Houston road past shops and restaurants.

One Twitter user messaged the racetrack with: 'What's up with the loose horse? Saw it from my car while at Target. Hope they find it safe!'

Gone: Joe has got rid of his jockey and is heading for freedom

Shopping: Minutes later Joseph was seen cantering past this Target store

Turfway Park tweeted back: 'A scrape or two, but he was corralled and is OK. Scary few moments for sure! He blew the 2nd turn and jumped the rail.'

Officials chasing the horse included the race-starter and a veterinarian and they were able to capture Joseph near the southbound I-75.

Fortunately they captured the runaway before he entered the interstate and no car accidents were reported. Joseph was transported back to the track in an equine ambulance.

Apart from wounded pride, neither the horse nor the jockey were hurt.
