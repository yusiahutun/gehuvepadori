Amal Clooney's make-up and hair how-to at Suburbicon premiere, Venice Film Festival

April 20, 2024
blog

Looking as perfectly polished as ever, Amal Clooney graced the Venice Film Festival red carpet with husband George this weekend for the premiere of Suburbicon.

Channelling an old-meets-new Hollywood look with a glossy faux bob and glowing complexion, her glam squad revealed the how-to behind it.

The A-list's favourite make-up artist, Charlotte Tilbury, used her own products–naturally–and revealed to us all the tricks of the trade.

The make-up

1.Tilbury prepped Amal's skin with her Instant Magic Facial Dry Sheet Mask, to reduce wrinkles, smooth, brighten, lift and hydrate the skin.

2.To achieve dewy, luminous skin, the make-up artist then applied her best-selling Magic Cream to Clooney's face and neck to plump and hydrate, and create the perfect canvas for make-up. She followed this with her Magic Eye Rescue to firm, plump and brighten the eye area.

3.Tilbury then applied her Magic Foundation in shades 5 and 7 for a flawless complexion.

4.She followed this with The Retoucher concealer in shade 3 and the Mini Miracle Eye Wand in 2 around the eyes and nose where needed, to mask any minor imperfections.

5.To enhance Clooney's cheekbones, Tilbury applied her new Hollywood Contour Wand in medium/dark for soft contours, buffed in with her Hollywood Complexion brush, and applied her new Hollywood Beauty Light Wand highlighter on top of her cheekbones, to bounce off the light.

6.To add subtle definition to Clooney's brows and structure to her face, Tilbury used her Brow Lift in Supermodel.

7.She then applied a wash of Eyes to Mesmerise cream eyeshadow in Marie Antoinette, lightly blended out towards the eye socket for guilded lids.

8.Rock 'n' Kohl liquid eyeliner pencil in Barbarella Brown was used to create a seductive feline flick, followed by Tilbury's Full Fat Lashes mascara on her top and bottom lashes.

9.She then applied Beachstick in Moonbeach, Las Salinas and Formentera on her cheeks, and cheekbones for a pretty, youthful glow.

10.Charlotte completed the look by mixing her Matte Revolution lipstick in Love Liberty (a berry matte pink) with The Queen (a rich, crown-jewel pink) to create a custom blend colour for Clooney.

11.And finally, she applied a light dusting of the Airbrush Flawless Finish Powder in shade 2, to perfect the skin, brighten the complexion and prevent excess shine.

The hair

Clooney's hairstylist Rod Ortega gave our sister site Harper's Bazaar US the exclusive rundown on exactly how he achieved her "1940s Italian glamour"-inspired faux bob look using T3 tools and some expert pinning.

1.Starting with wet hair, he combed through two pumps of the Rene Furterer Karite Leave in Nourishing Cream from the mid-sections to the ends of Clooney's hair. He then used a paddle brush to distribute the cream evenly.

2.Before blow-drying, Ortega blotted her hair to remove excess moisture. "This is a very important pre-blow dry step," he explained.

3.Using the T3 Featherweight Luxe 2i dryer and the 2.5" Ibiza Hair round brush, he started blow-drying her hair starting in the back and working around all the way to the front, smoothing each section as he did so.

Getty Images

4.Once dry, Ortega used the T3 Voluminous Hair Curling Iron (1.5"). Starting with the vertical sections around the face, from the ear to the top of the head, he then pinned each curl as he went. He used bigger sections to curl the back for looser waves which were then pinned up as well.

5.Once completely pinned and cooled, the hair stylist applied some strong hold hairspray before unpinning all of the back curls, brushing out the waves, and then backcombing them.

6.Next, he took a chignon foam cut in half. "Using the half, I took it to the ends of the hair and rolled it up to the head, pinning it underneath," he explained. "I released the front section, brushed it out, backcombed it and tucked it underneath, pinning it to the chignon form."

7.To finish the look, Ortega pinned the hair behind the ear on Clooney's left and applied a light spritz of hairspray, moulding her hair into the final wavy shape.
