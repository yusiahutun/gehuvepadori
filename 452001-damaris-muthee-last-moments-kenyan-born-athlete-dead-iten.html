New details show that the slain Kenyan-born athlete Damaris Muthee Mutua trained with her teammates in Kapsabet, Nandi County, on Saturday, April 16.

Four days later, the lifeless body of the 24-year-old was found in a house in Iten, Elgeyo Marakwet county, over 80km from her training camp.

The Arab Cross Country silver medallist was reportedly lured to the death trap by her Ethiopian lover, a fellow athlete identified as Eskinder Hailemaryam Folie.

The following day, Sunday, April 17, her coach Gregory Kilonzo said she did not show up for breakfast.

“It was the same at lunchtime. I thought the girl was still resting or maybe she had gone to church or to the shop. In the evening, we again did not see her. I tried calling her cell phone but it did not go through. I asked her friends to do the same, so everybody called but calls did not go through,” said Kilonzo as quoted by Nation.

The deceased did not show up for the training on Monday, April 18.

Kilonzo said they also looked for her in her other training camp in Iten, but she was not there. Only clothes were found in her house.

Body found in boyfriend's house

On Tuesday, April 19, the coach said they got information that a female athlete had been found dead in a house in Iten. She would letter be identified to be Muthee.

A close source intimated to TUKO.co.ke that the deceased and her boyfriend were on a rough phase in their relationship, which is believed to have led to her untimely demise.

Folie reportedly fled from the country after the incident, which police suspected ensued on Sunday, April 17.

He later called the deceased's kin to inform them that she had committed suicide, further revealing to them where the body was lying.

"The boyfriend called her and she acceded to her advances. He killed her and immediately left the country. He used a different number to call the deceased's kin informing them of where the body was lying," said the source.
