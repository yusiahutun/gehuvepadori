Officials Find KSh 165 Million in Account of Primary School Teacher with KSh 23,000 Salary

April 01, 2024
blog
  • A primary school teacher has been made to forfeit millions of shillings found in her bank accounts by the Independent Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Commission
  • The commission had said that it found a total of KSh 165,618,540 in the account of the teacher, whose salary is a mere KSh 23,300
  • According to ICPC, Egbuha worked with some individuals and a private-owned company to divert the proceeds of the crime after she was caught

The Independent Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Commission (ICPC) on Thursday, July 28, announced that it found a total of KSh 165,618,540 in the account of a public primary school teacher with a salary of KSh 23,300.

Premium Times reports that the Federal High Court sitting in Abuja has also ordered the final forfeiture of KSh 36,800,000 traced to the teacher, Roseline Egbuha, who teaches at Ozala Primary School, Abagana, Anambra State.

A statement by the commission's spokesperson, Azuka Ogugua, said that the court headed by Justice D.U Okorowo also ordered the forfeiture of cars traced to Egbuha and some of her accomplices in money laundering activities.

Details of court order against Egbuha and others

Ogugua noted that the court had issued its order on June 26 and 27, but the commission's lawyer was only able to obtain the Certified True Copy on Wednesday, July 27.

According to the ICPC, the court's order is a sequel to an ex-parte motion filed by the commission in 2021.

The motion also followed an allegation surrounding KSh 165 million linked to Egbuha and her accomplices who tried to hide the funds in her Guaranty Trust Bank account.

ICPC's action against corrupt practices

Investigations by the commission also prompted the ICPC to place a Post-No-Debit on her account, which led to her obtaining a court order from the Federal High Court, Abuja, to lift the PND placed on the account.

It was gathered that the accused immediately withdrew the funds and distributed them to multiple accounts owned by Bureau de Change owners and some other private individuals, including James Erebouye, Emon Okune and Chisom Iwueke.

Others are Alonge Ojo, Maureen Chidimma, Owoyemi Mayowa and Ejeaka Ifeoma, and two private companies charged to court over the alleged crimes' fraudulent activities and warehousing proceeds.

Other items forfeited by the respondents are cars which include Toyota Lexus and Venza sport utility vehicles.

In a related incidence reported by TUKO.co.ke, The Ethics Anti-Corruption Commission obtained orders from the High Court to freeze KSh 18 million belonging to Tracy Njoki King’e, a junior employee at the National Treasury.

According to EACC, 30-year-old Njoki joined Treasury in January 2020 as a Finance Officer II from the Ministry of Industry.

She had been employed at the Ministry of Industry, Trade and Cooperatives- State Department of Cooperatives since 2018.

Njoki earned a monthly salary of KSh 50,954.53 between March 2020 and May 2022, which amounted to approximately KSh 1,350,000 during the period under investigation.

“Between February 2020 and March 2022, Tracy Njoki King’e received Ksh 24,119,926.00 as imprests, facilitation and extraneous allowance in her Cooperative bank account No. 01116124598700 while between February 2022 and June 2022, Tracy Njoki received KSh 11,899,052.00 as imprests, facilitation and extraneous allowance in her I&M bank account No. 00403523442050,” EACC statement read.
