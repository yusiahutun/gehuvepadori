Kameelah Williams, (born March 8, 1978) commonly known as Meelah, is an American recording artist best known for being the lead singer of the famous Motown 1990s trio “702". 702 consisted of Williams and sisters LeMisha and Irish Grinstead, who were students at the Las Vegas Academy of Performing Arts. Thanks to the help and support of actor/comedian Sinbad and recording artist Michael Bivins, the girls were discovered. They made their recording debut on Subway's hit single "This Lil' Game We Play".
