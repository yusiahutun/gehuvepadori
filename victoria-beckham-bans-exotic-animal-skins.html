Victoria Beckham will no longer use exotic animal skins in her collections

February 18, 2024
blog

Getty

Victoria Beckham will no longer use exotic animal skins in her collections. The designer – whose brand was already fur-free – will adopt this new policy with her autumn/winter 2019 collection, which will be shown in the next few days during London Fashion Week.

"Victoria Beckham will cease the use of exotic skins in all collections as of AW19," a statement from the brand read. "The Victoria Beckham brand has never used fur in its clothing or accessories collections and confirmed last year that the brand will remain fur free."

Tristan Fewings/BFC//Getty Images

Victoria Beckham is the latest in a string of designers to adopt this new way of working. Vivienne Westwood, Chanel and Diane von Furstenberg have all pledged not to use exotic animal skins in future collections, meanwhile Gucci, Burberry, Michael Kors, Versace, Ralph Lauren, Calvin Klein and many more have all also gone fur-free.

"Behind every crocodile, alligator, snake, or lizard handbag or pair of shoes is a violent death," PETA Director Elisa Allen said in a statement. "Victoria Beckham's decision to ban exotic skins will spare countless remarkable animals immense suffering, and PETA calls on other luxury brands to follow her kind example."
