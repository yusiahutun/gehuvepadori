Narok is a diverse county with rich culture, land, resources, and people. The county is approximately 18,000 square kilometers and has a population of over 1.2 million permanent residents across its six sub-counties.

The dominant ethnic group in Narok County is the Maasai, and its capital and largest town is Narok. Another prominent urban center is Kilgoris. Narok County address code is 20500, and Narok County postal address is P.O Box 898-20500 Narok, Kenya.

Narok South postal code

Narok South is one of the largest constituencies in Narok County. It was established for the 1966 elections. The constituency borders the Republic of Tanzania to the south and Nakuru County to the north.

Narok South has six wards: Sogoo, Sagamian, Melelo, Ololulunga, Maji-moto/ Naroosura, and Loita. Its postal address is P.O Box 46682 00100 Nairobi, Kenya, or you can call them at +254 709 894000.

Narok East postal code

Narok East constituency was created for the 2013 general election. It borders Kajiado and Nakuru Counties. It is constituency number 180 and has a total population of approximately 82,956.

It's divided into four wards: Suswa, Keekonyokie, IIdamat, and Mosiro. Narok East postal address is P.O Box 41842 00100, Nairobi, Kenya, or you can call them at +254-20-2221291.

Narok West postal code

Narok West is one of the constituencies of Narok County. It has four wards: Naikara, Siana, IImotiok, and Mara. The postal address for Narok West P.O. Box is 4662-00100, or call +254 709 894000.

Narok North postal code

Narok North, a constituency under Narok County, has a population of 175,000. The constituency is sparsely populated because the majority of its people are pastoralists. It's divided into six wards: Melili, Nkareta, Olorropil, Narok town, Olokurto, and Olposimoru. Narok North's postal code is 20500.

Frequently asked questions

  • What are the sub-counties of Narok County? The sub-counties of Narok County are Narok North, Emurua Dikirr, Narok West, Narok East, Kilgoris, and Narok South.
  • What is Narok County known for? Narok County is home to the Maasai Mara national reserve, one of its primary sources of economic income. The county is also famous for wheat farming, that's done in both large and small-scale farming.
  • What river is found in Narok County? The famous river in Narok County is the Mara River, which begins in Narok County (Kenya) and ends in Mara Region (Tanzania). The river lies across the ungulates migration path in the Maasai Mara/ Serengeti ecosystem.
  • What crops do well in Narok? The crop that does well in Narok is wheat. However, farmers grow various crops, including yams, cassava, sorghum, maize, etc.
  • What is the meaning of Narok? The meaning of Narok is black or dark water. The county natives are mostly Maasai, who call it Enkare Narok, the name of the river flowing through Narok Town.
