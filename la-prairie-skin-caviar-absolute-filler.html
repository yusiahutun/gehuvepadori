La Prairie Skin Caviar Absolute Filler Review

April 18, 2024
blog

As someone who's had fillers injected into my face multiple times, I know one thing to be true: fillers are as expensive as they are painful. You could even say they hurt your Amex bill more than your face. A single syringe of cheek-or-lip-plumping Restylane or Juvéderm can cost anywhere from $600 to $1,000 depending on your doctor and where you live. Then they only last about a year (or in my unlucky case, four months, thanks to how quickly I dissolve hyaluronic acid).

I present all this information about fillers upfront so that when I tell you how much the product I am recommending costs, you don't immediately descend on my apartment building with flaming pitchforks. It's a skin cream—and it costs a staggering $590. But the La Prairie Skin Caviar Absolute Filler moisturizer promises to plump and enhance aging skin much like filler, but without any poking or needles. All things considered, it's one of the most luxurious and effective moisturizers I've ever slathered on my skin—even if the price might cause a few wrinkles of its own—and I'll tell you why.

Let's talk science. Skin Caviar Absolute Filler is the first product from the caviar-obsessed Swiss brand to use a brand-new ingredient called Caviar Absolute. "We knew that our extraction process was good as we were pulling out a number of caviar components into the solvents. But it also heats the caviar, so we imagined that some of the components were not as pristine as they could be. But extract without heat and you get less components into the solvent, which is not the way we wanted to go," says Dr. Jacqueline Hill, La Prairie's Director of Strategic Innovation and Science. "Over the course of four years, Hill are her team began to look at other technologies and methods to extract caviar like distillation, which they used in last year's Essence-in-Lotion. What they eventually landed on was centrifugation—or spinning something really, really fast until it breaks apart. "Centrifugation is not often used in creating cosmetics. It was always interesting to look at techniques from other areas and see if we can apply them," says Hill. "It did allow us to bring out the caviar oils and proteins and separate them from non-functional components. It’s a gentle way. There's no heating involved, no solvents or chemical elements, nothing that would make us lose the benefits [of caviar].”

With a cleaner, more potent caviar oil and protein mix called Caviar Absolute, Hill and her team were able to develop Absolute Filler, a moisturizer that boosts your own cell's collagen and hyaluronic acid production from the inside-out (it also contains anti-aging powerhouses brown algae and peony root extract). Now, plenty of creams claim to do the ability to plump for a whole lot less. But what made be a believer was this formula's ability to make my skin look fresher right away, not just over time. That has to do with the moisturizer's unusual texture: a whipped consistency that's fluffier and bouncier than homemade whipped cream. It truly looks (and smells!) good enough to eat.

The way the cream is delivered is perhaps the coolest part. Dermatologists, cosmetic chemists, and beauty junkies alike have long complained about the pitfalls of jar packaging. An open jar of cream might look beautiful, but it exposes the formula and all its precious ingredients to oxidation and germs. Absolute Filler comes in a jar with a flip-lid and an airtight pump mechanism. You simply push down on the top of the jar and the perfect caviar-bead-sized amount dispenses through the top. It's almost too much fun watching the little bead come to the surface. Please, don't waste any product for a Boomerang. One pump is enough for your face while a second pump can cover your neck and decollate.

The plush formula is meant to be applied over your entire face so that you achieve a "harmonious" balance of plumpness. It's being over-filled in one particular area—the cheekbones, the lips—that makes faces look done. But if you're noticing volume loss in just one area, like under your orbital bones, Hill notes that you can apply the cream to just that one area, too. "One of the reasons we find inspiration in the area of cosmeceuticals is because it's filling a need. We are always looking at what women and men's concerns are," says Hill. "It's clear that if you're going for injections then wrinkles and volume loss are a concern. We're not trying to match surgery because it's invasive, we can't compare the results of topical application with surgery. Though that would be the golden standard—to get a similar effect."

You'll notice your skin feels bouncier right after the first time you use it, but it takes about a month before you'll catch a glimpse of your skin in the morning and wonder why you look so fresh. This cream is why. Now, it won't replace injectables entirely, particularly if you've already experienced major volume loss. If you have a little wrinkle here or there (20 to-30-year-olds, we're talking to you), then this cream's effects will be subtle. Mature and drier skin types will notice the biggest change by adding this to their bathroom shelf. But I particularly love it as a supplement to an existing injectable routine. "It should be a good complement," says Hill. "And you might be able to delay the next injection somewhat."
