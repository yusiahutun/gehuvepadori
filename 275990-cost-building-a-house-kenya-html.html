4. Fittings

The fittings of a building include mirrors, curtains, bathroom fittings, fitted carpets, freestanding fridges, fitted kitchens, lighting fittings, and fitted cupboards. Note that the painting is not among the fittings.

5. Types of joinery

Joinery is essential because it allows one to join two boards to create a solid fashion. 6. Specification of materials

What workmanship and materials do you need to develop the building? Specifications of materials include the drawings, quantities, and schedules for building a house. All these add up to the cost of the construction.

7. Floor-to-ceiling heights

The more you request the ceiling height to be high, the more you should get prepared to dig deeper into your pocket. The most preferred ceiling size is approximately 7 feet to 9 feet because it makes a house appear smaller, less accommodating, and more cramped.

8. Site topography

Site topography will determine the base of the building to support it. If a site is flat, the topography may not influence the location and layout of the building, but on a sloping site, the topography is likely to be a significant design factor.

9. Wall-to-floor ratio

The wall-to-floor ratio expresses the number of external walls per unit of floor area and thus is a critical measure of cost-efficiency. This ratio typically accounts for 15 to 25% of construction costs. There are some calculations that will be required to be done to determine the wall-to-floor ratio of your building which will cost a significant sum.
