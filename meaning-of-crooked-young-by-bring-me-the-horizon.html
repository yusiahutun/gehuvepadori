Meaning of Crooked Young by Bring Me The Horizon

February 03, 2024
blog

The overall theme of Bring Me The Horizon's song "Crooked Young" is a critique of organized religion and the oppressive nature of faith. The lyrics delve into themes of rebellion, self-reliance, and the rejection of traditional beliefs. This article aims to explore the multiple standout lyrics in the song that help develop unexpected and imaginative themes while tying back to the overarching theme.One standout lyric that captures the essence of the song is, "Fuck your faith, no one's gonna save you." This lyric challenges the idea of blindly placing one's trust in a higher power or religious institution. It encourages individuals to rely on themselves rather than seeking salvation or guidance from external sources. Building on this theme, the line "Believe in no one but yourself" emphasizes the importance of self-belief and empowering individuals to trust their own instincts and abilities.Another striking lyric that contributes to the overall theme is, "We don't sleep, we don't eat, we speak in tongues." This lyric suggests a state of heightened consciousness and spiritual awakening. Instead of conforming to societal norms and indulging in mundane pursuits like sleep and food, the crooked young prioritize exploring their own minds and questioning the established order. It also portrays the idea of speaking in tongues as a symbol of breaking free from societal constraints and expressing oneself freely.The chorus lyrics, "There's no hope for us, we speak in tongues, blacker than the sun, no death can't touch the crooked young," introduce a mystical and enigmatic quality to the song. These lines suggest that the crooked young embrace their differences and reject the notion of hopelessness imposed upon them by society. They find strength in their unique perspectives and experiences, symbolized by speaking in tongues and being "blacker" than the sun. This could be interpreted as a rebellion against the traditional understanding of light and darkness, suggesting that true power lies within the unconventional and marginalized.One of the final lyrics, "The clouds won't hear your fucking prayers," serves as a scathing critique of religious rituals and their perceived efficacy. It challenges the notion that prayers are answered and highlights the disillusionment faced by those who have placed their faith in an external deity. This lyric reinforces the overall theme by emphasizing the importance of self-reliance and rejecting the idea of relying on higher powers for salvation or help.In summary, "Crooked Young" by Bring Me The Horizon critiques organized religion and emphasizes the importance of self-belief and rebellion against societal norms. The standout lyrics explored in this article help develop unexpected and imaginative themes, such as the rejection of blind faith, the pursuit of self-discovery, and finding power in perceived darkness. Ultimately, the song encourages individuals to question traditional beliefs and rely on their own instincts and abilities.
