Young girl dies after being thrown from 'exploding bouncy castle on Norfolk beach'

February 29, 2024
blog

A police cordon has been put in place around a play area at Gorleston beach in Norfolk, Great Yarmouth, after the "serious incident" on Sunday afternoon.

Emergency services were called to the beach after shocked eyewitnesses called 999 multiple times at around 11am this morning.

The bouncy castle "exploded" while the youngster was on it, throwing her "20 ft in the air", eyewitnesses have said.

The girl, whose name and age is not yet known, was taken to James Paget Hospital in a "serious medical condition".

She later died in hospital, police confirmed in a statement several hours later.

One eyewitness wrote on social media: "Inflatable trampoline just exploded on Gorleston beach with children on it."

Kara Longshaw, who saw the aftermath of the accident, claims paramedics performed CRP on the girl for 15 minutes before taking her to hospital.

In a post on Facebook, she wrote: "Just seen the most horrific thing in my life. A bouncy castle exploded at the beach and the child on it was catapulted about 20ft into the air.

"Please say a prayer for the 4-year-old being rushed to hospital after 15 minutes of CPR."

The accident happened as temperatures soared into the high 20sC on Gorleston beach, a popular area usually thronged by hundreds of sunseekers when the weather is nice.

Images taken of the scene show a police cordon around what appears to be crumpled deck chairs.

But the beach has not been closed after the accident, with sunbathers visible in the background.

In a statement, Norfolk police said: "Police can confirm a child has sadly died following an incident in Gorleston earlier today, Sunday 1 July 2018.

"Officers were called to Lower Esplanade around 11.15am following reports a child had been thrown from a bouncy castle.

"The young girl was taken to the James Paget Hospital; however sadly died.

"Next of kin have been informed and are being supported by specialist officers."

A spokesman for the East of England Ambulance Service said: "We attended the incident this morning at Gorleston beach, involving a patient using inflatable equipment.

"Several of our teams were dispatched, with the first on the scene in four minutes.

"The young female was seriously injured and in cardiac arrest on our arrival and was conveyed to James Paget Hospital. Sadly, despite all the of the efforts and interventions, she was pronounced deceased.

"We would like to thank everyone who rushed to respond the young patient and did everything possible to give her treatment and care. Our thoughts are with the family at this time."

The girl's next of kin have been informed and are being supported by specialist officers.

A joint investigation between the Health and Safety Executive (HSE), local authority and police has been launched to establish the circumstances surrounding the incident.

The incident comes two years after Summer Grant was killed when a bouncy castle she was on was carried away by a gust of wind.

The seven-year-old died when the inflatable, which had not been properly secured, was blown 300 metres down a hill at an Easter fair in Harlow, Essex, in March 2016.

Married fairground workers William Thurston, 29, and Shelby Thurston, 26 were jailed for three years over the incident.
