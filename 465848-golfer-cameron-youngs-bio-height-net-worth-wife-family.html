Early life

Cameron Young was born on May 7, 1997, in Briarcliff Manor, New York. He is the son of David and Barbara Young, who are actively involved in the pro golf community. David Young is the director of golf at Sleepy Hollow Country Club and an active golfer. On the other hand, Barbara Young is a former golfer who once wanted to play in the LPGA.

Cameron landed the Lanny Wadkins Scholarship at Wake Forest University and was part of the school's golf team. He had also competed for the Fordham Prep team while in high school.

Junior career

Cameron started his golf career by participating in the M.G.A. Junior golf championships. In 2011 (at 15 years), he competed in and won the Carter Cup. He continued competing in the scene and was named the M.G.A. Player of the Year in 2015. He also competed in the 2014 U.S. Amateur Championship, reaching the quarterfinals stage.

In 2014, he participated in the Junior P.G.A. Championship, finishing sixth. Aside from individual competitions, Cameron also represented Fordham Prep's golf team. On top of that, he was part of the Team U.S.A. in the 2014 Junior Ryder Cup and the Junior World Cup.

College career

In college, Cameron competed for the Wake Forest golf team. In his first year, he played in 13 events, including the U.S. Collegiate Championship, where he emerged the champion. He qualified for the U.S. Amateur Championship in his sophomore year, winning the 2017 New York Open.

Senior career

Cameron started his senior career in 2020 when he qualified for the Korn Ferry Tour, a developmental tour for the U.S. Pro Golf Association. After posting an impressive performance, he was allowed into the tour for the rest of the 2020/21 season. In 2021, he won four consecutive Korn Ferry Tour events.
