Man charges at police officer with rifle near Denver

February 04, 2024
blog

The horrifying moment a police officer was attacked by a suspect with a semi-automatic rifle during a routine traffic stop has been captured on camera.

Douglas County sheriff’s deputy Brad Proulx, who has been with the force for six years, stopped a white SUV on County Line Road and Santa Fe Drive near Denver on Friday night.

Proulx had got out of his cruiser and walked up to the passenger side window, before he turned to walk around the back and approach the driver's door.

However, the footage from the cop's shows the man inside the car, Deyon Marcus Rivas-Maestas, charge at him.

Rivas-Maestas attempted to hit Proulx with his gun - which was not loaded, according to Littleton Police Department.

Proulx fell to the ground and fired two shots, hitting the 25-year-old suspect in the arm once.

The cop then got to his feet and held the suspect at gunpoint for about four minutes until further officers arrived and made the arrest. 

Douglas County police released footage of the shocking moment, saying it was a prime example of what its officers are trained to do before they hit the streets.

'The result of this shooting is a testament to the training each of our deputies are provided,' Sheriff Tony Spurlock said.

'This just shows how necessary it is for deputies to be able to make split-second decisions, not only for their own protection but for the protection of the community.'

Deputy Steven Johnson told the Denver Post the incident at first played out like a scene every officer dreads.

'I speak for every law enforcement officer: When you hear "Shots fired. Shots fired", it’s the worst thing that can happen',' Johnson said.

'Deputy Proux - amazing. It’s nothing more than amazing that he was able to defend himself.'

Proulx was unharmed in the incident, and is on administrative leave. He has told his superiors he is ready to return to work. 

Rivas-Maestas was taken to hospital and treated for a gunshot wound to the arm. 

He was released Wednesday and then booked into Douglas County jail, and has been charged with first-degree assault.
