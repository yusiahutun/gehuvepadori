Popular Facebook writer Ruth James has come out to defend Pastor Ezekiel Odero following his arrest over the alleged indoctrination and deaths of his church members.

Ruth, a follower of Pastor Ezekiel, said that through the founder of New Life Church, she got saved from drug addiction.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Ruth said her life changed for the better after attending Ezekiel's past crusade in Kisumu that saw thousands of faithful flock to the event for healing and deliverance.

"After fasting for three days at Kirembe the last time he had the crusade in Kisumu, I quit drugs and alcohol addiction that had been killing me for the past three years. I no longer drink alcohol," she said.

After his arrest, controversial stories about the man of God have been making rounds on social media, and the writer defended her spiritual father, saying he was a true man of God.

" I still believe that he is up to no evil," she insisted.

Ezekiel is known for praying for witch doctors to die and 'sending' bedbugs to infest the homes of side chicks/concubines, popularly known as mpango wa kando, and that always leaves many questioning his teachings.

Defending Ezekiel, Ruth supported the televangelist's teachings, and she said:

"I support him 100%. Mpango wa kando atafute wake peke yake, si wa mtu."

Still on the preacher's teachings, she said:

"He is teaching us to avoid immorality that is why he hates mpango wa kando. He encourages people to get married and stick to it, which is something that everyone would wish to experience."

Pastor Sarah speaks following Ezekiel's arrest

Earlier, TUKO.co.ke reported that Pastor Sarah asked congregants to stay prayerful after Ezekiel was arrested.

At the time of his arrest this morning, a church service was ongoing in his Kilifi mega-church, and it was also being aired on the church's social media platforms.

His wife took to the podium to announce to the church what had happened and said they will not hold services for the next few days to allow for investigations.
