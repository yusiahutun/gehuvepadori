Sam McKnight's favourite hair products and tools

February 22, 2024
blog

Perhaps the most influential hairdresser of our times, Sam McKnight has the hands behind many an iconic hairstyle of the past 40 years; from Princess Diana's headline-stealing cuts to Kate Moss's wedding day waves and Kendall Jenner and Gigi Hadid's colour swap on the catwalk. Now, for the first time, 'brand Sam' has been brought to retail, with the release of Hair by Sam McKnight, launching with four dry styling products that deliver hair a dose of front-cover cool.

The efficacious products are the results of McKnight's extensive experience both backstage and on shoots, and are clearly his new kit bag heroes. In addition to these, the legend shared with us the tools he can't work without. Consider this the ultimate hairstyling lust list:

Cool Girl – Think Kate in a can. "An effortless, barely there texturiser that creates style that lasts all day," McKnight explains.

Philip Kingsley Elasticizer – A beauty classic. This award-winning hair strengthener is the ultimate moisture shot for strands.

Bumble & Bumble Surf Spray – A trendsetting styling product that is often imitated. The salt spray adds texture and body to wavy and limp locks.

Lazy Girl – McKnight's "ultimate dry-shampoo", which he also says is a styling product in its own right.

Magic Move Pomades – An oldie but a goldie. This Japanese styling pomade comes in a Soft, Light and Hard formula letting McKnight create a multitude of looks.

Easy Up-Do – Like invisible hairgrips, McKnight explains that this "creates that nonchalant yet glamorous up-do that all girls want."

BaByliss hairdryer – "A good blow dryer and pair of curling tongs" are the heat styling tools McKnight swears by and BaByliss is his go-to brand.

Y.S.Park hairbrushes – Although McKnight admits his hands are his best tool, he adds "a great hairbrush and some pins" are essential. His top choice are the brushes designed by Japanese hair guru and stylist Y.S.Park – a cult favourite for good reason.

Modern Hairspray – This fine hairspray has a brushable formula, which McKnight says "allows for mistakes". Suits us.
