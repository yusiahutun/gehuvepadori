Career and Professional Life

Jaime Munguia started his professional career in the year 2013. Before facing Sadam All for the WBO junior middleweight title on May 12, 2018, he had a 28-0 record. The victory gave him his second career title after the WBC Latino light middleweight championship.

On July 21, 2018, he defeated former light-middleweight champion Liam Smith by unanimous decision in his first defense of the WBO junior middleweight belt. On September 15, 2018, he successfully defended his world title against Brandon Cook in the third round via technical knockout on the undercard of Canelo Alvarez vs Gennady Golovkin II.

He defended his title against Dennis Hogan on April 13, 2019. He reigned in a controversial majority vote. Additionally, he successfully defended his WBO light-middleweight title for the fifth time after defeating Patrick Allotey on September 14, 2019, in Carson, California, with a technical knockout in the fourth round.

He faced Gary O’Sullivan on January 11 2020 and defeated O’Sullivan via technical knockout in the eleventh round. He defeated Tureano Johnson on October 30, 2020, to win the WBO Intercontinental middleweight championship. On June 19, 2021, he defeated Kamil Szeremeta to defend his title.
