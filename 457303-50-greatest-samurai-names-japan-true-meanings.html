Legendary samurai names

Samurai were known for their ability to use swords, bows and arrows, and spears. Here are some of the deadliest Samurai names.

  • Miyamoto Musashi - A famous and accomplished soldier, swordsman, and artist. He existed between 1584 and 1645.
  • Date Masamune - Founder of the city of Sendai. He was an incredible tactician.
  • Minamoto no Yoshitsune - He led the Genpei War between the Taira and Minamoto clans.
  • Tokugawa Ieyasu - He was the son of the lord of Mikawa province. He led his clan when the civil war broke out and won, thus earning the title Shogun. He was born in 1543 and died in 1616.
  • Toyotomi Hideyoshi - He served as chief Imperial minister. He helped complete the 16th-century unification of Japan.
  • Date Masamune - Young and fearless Samurai who led his first campaign at age 14 and conquered most of the Tohoku region by age 22.
  • Tomoe Gozen - A female Samurai who defeated Yoritomo’s strongest warrior in the battle of Awazu.
  • Yamagata Masakage - One of the 'Twenty-Four Generals Of Takeda Shingen'.
  • Kusunoki Masashige - He was famous for his military strategies and unfailing devotion.
  • Oda Nobunaga - Believed to be responsible for the unification of the nations during the Warring States period in Japan.
  • Takeda Shingen - Belonged to the Takeda clan that descended from the Minamoto clan. Shingen was called the 'Tiger Of Kai'.
  • Hattori Hanzo - He was known to be a fearless warrior and performed many feats in service of the clan.
  • Watanabe no Tsuna - One of the earliest Samurai class members to be immortalised in Japanese legend.
  • Shimazu Yoshihisa - Considered one of the most outstanding leaders of his time, Shimazu Yoshihisa was lord of the Satsuma Province.
  • Honda Tadakatsu - A loyal servant of Tokugawa Ieyasu, Honda Tadakatsu was a key figure in many of Ieyasu’s battles.

  • Musashi (Brave Fencer Musashi) - He can learn new combat techniques from the townsfolk he is trying to liberate.
